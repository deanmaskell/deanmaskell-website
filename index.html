Does This Sound Familiar?

Something is working. You have a product, a brand, or a service that people genuinely want. You found a channel — paid social, Google, word of mouth, a partnership relationship — and it delivered. For a while, that was enough.

But it has hit a ceiling. The cost of acquiring each customer has crept up to the point where the economics are getting uncomfortable. The channel that carried you here cannot carry you to the next level at the same cost. And now you're starting to ask: what do I do next?

The answer isn't obvious. Add a second channel? Fix sales conversion? Improve retention? Each voice in the room has a different opinion. Your marketing agency is focused on their metrics. Your junior marketing person is doing their best but doesn't have the seniority to join the dots. Your finance director is looking at the P&L and the marketing numbers don't reconcile with what they see.

And you — as the founder — are being pulled in every direction. Brand. Product. Stock. Operations. Investors. You cannot own the growth problem yourself. But you also cannot hand it to someone who doesn't understand the commercial outcome you're trying to reach.

The real problem isn't the channel. It's that nobody owns the growth equation end to end.

There is no single person accountable for the commercial outcome of all of it. No one is joining the dots between what the agencies are doing, what the numbers actually mean, and what needs to happen next to move the business forward.

That gap is what I fill.

What Changes?

Not a list of deliverables. What your world actually looks like.

One version of the truth

Marketing, finance, and your agencies are all working from the same numbers — reconciled to the P&L. The arguments stop. Decisions get made faster.

A clear growth priority

Instead of five competing opinions, there are two or three things that will actually move the needle — identified, validated, and agreed. Everyone knows what they're working towards and why.

Agencies that are directed, not directing

Your marketing agency, your Google / Meta Ads agency, and your email team each have a clear brief tied to commercial outcomes. They are being held accountable to business performance — not their own platform metrics.

A founder back in control

You are not managing the detail. You are being updated on what matters, making the calls that are yours to make, and spending your energy on the things only you can do.

Something that runs without you

When I leave, I leave you with something that works — frameworks, reporting, processes, a team that understands what good looks like. Not a deck. Not a plan that gathers dust. A clock that keeps ticking.

What I don't do

I am not a channel specialist. I will not be running your Meta Ads or writing your email campaigns. I am not the right person if you already have a marketing director or systems that are already established and just need minimal optimising.

Why Fractional

Hiring a senior growth or marketing director takes time you probably don't have. The recruitment process alone typically runs three to six months. A Head of Ecommerce or Growth Director at a brand your size will cost £80,000 to £110,000 in base salary — and once you add a recruitment agency fee of 20–25%, employer National Insurance at 13.8%, pension contributions and benefits, the true cost in year one is well over £130,000.
